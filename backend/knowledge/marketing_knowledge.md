## Song Analysis — How to Analyze a Track

When analyzing a song from a YouTube link, video file, or transcript, always cover these points:

- Genre and subgenre
- Mood and energy level (low / mid / high energy)
- Target audience: age range, interests, and which platforms they use
- Best platform to lead the release campaign
- TikTok and Reels hook potential: identify the strongest 15 to 30 second moment and explain why it works as a hook

If a music video is provided, also identify:
- 3 to 5 visually strong moments that could work as Reels or TikTok clips
- Overall visual mood and aesthetic of the video
- Any moment that has viral potential and why it would resonate

STRICT RULE: Base analysis only on provided content. Never invent streaming statistics, chart positions, or listener counts.

## Timeline Reality Check — Minimum Days Required Before Release

When an artist gives a release date, immediately calculate whether it is realistic. Use these minimum timelines:

- Cover art creation: minimum 5 days
- Distributor upload and platform confirmation: 3 to 7 days depending on distributor
  - DistroKid: approximately 1 to 3 days
  - TuneCore: approximately 3 to 5 days
  - Agata: approximately 3 to 5 days (recommended for Lithuanian artists, free option)
- Spotify editorial pitch: must be submitted minimum 7 days before release date. Strongly recommended to submit 4 weeks (28 days) before release for best chance of editorial consideration.
- Music video production if needed: minimum 30 days
- ABSOLUTE MINIMUM timeline with no video and no Spotify pitch: 14 days from today
- RECOMMENDED MINIMUM timeline that allows Spotify pitch: 28 days from today
- WITH music video included: minimum 35 days from today

If the release date is too soon, say clearly: "This timeline is too tight. I recommend [specific date] as a realistic release date because [reason]." Never proceed with an unrealistic plan without flagging this first and recommending a better date.

## Press Release — How to Write One

Before writing the press release, ask the artist these five questions:
1. What is the story behind this song? What moment or feeling inspired it?
2. What do you want listeners to feel when they hear it?
3. Have you released music before? If yes, what are the most notable previous releases?
4. What is one thing about you as an artist that makes you unique?
5. Are there any collaborators, producers, or featured artists to mention?

Press release structure:
- Headline: artist name, song title, release date
- Release date and distributor information
- Song description based on the analysis
- Artist quote (only use what the artist provides — mark missing quotes as [ADD ARTIST QUOTE HERE])
- Background paragraph about the artist
- Streaming links (use placeholder [STREAMING LINK HERE])
- Press contact details (use placeholder [PRESS CONTACT HERE])

STRICT RULE: Never invent quotes. Only use exact words the artist provides. Mark every missing piece of information with [ADD THIS].

## Spotify Editorial Pitch — How to Write One

Write a Spotify editorial pitch description that meets all these requirements:
- Written in third person
- Describes the mood, genre, and sonic feel of the song
- Explains the story or emotion behind the song
- Mentions the target playlist mood or genre category it fits
- Under 500 characters total (this is Spotify's hard limit)
- Does not use hype words like amazing, incredible, groundbreaking, or game-changing
- Reads as if a professional playlist editor wrote it, not a publicist

Always remind the artist:
- The pitch must be submitted through Spotify for Artists, not by email
- Must be submitted minimum 7 days before release date
- Only one song per release can be pitched
- Pitching 4 weeks early significantly increases chances of editorial playlist consideration

## Radio Submission — Full Protocol

### Timing
Send the night before release at 18:00–20:00 local time. This hits
morning-shift inboxes first when programmers are planning their day.
If your release is Friday June 11th, send Thursday June 10th at 19:00.

### File Format Priority
1. WAV (primary — required for broadcast quality)
2. MP3 320kbps (backup and preview copy)
Always include high-resolution cover art (JPG or PNG) in the same folder.

### BCC Requirement
Put YOUR OWN email in the "To:" field.
Put ALL station emails in the "BCC:" field.
This is mandatory — stations must not see each other's addresses.

### Asset Folder Setup
Before sending, create one permanent shareable folder
(Google Drive or Dropbox) containing:
1. ArtistName_SongTitle_WAV
2. ArtistName_SongTitle_320kbps
3. Cover art — high-resolution JPG or PNG
4. Radio edit — clean version if original contains profanity
5. Short bio — 2 to 3 sentence PDF about the artist

### Submission Email Template
Subject: [Genre] - [Artist Name] - [Song Title] (Official Radio Submission)

Hi [Name/Music Director],

I'm reaching out to share my new single "[Song Title]",
which officially releases tomorrow, [Release Date].

I've included the high-quality WAV for broadcast and the cover art
in the link below. Based on your programming, I believe this would
be a great fit for your upcoming shows.

Stream/Preview:
- Spotify: [SPOTIFY LINK]
- YouTube: [YOUTUBE LINK]

Download Audio and Assets: [LINK TO FOLDER]

Quick Info:
- Artist: [Artist Name]
- Track: [Song Title]
- Vibe: [1-sentence description]
- ISRC: [Insert Code]

Thank you for supporting independent music!

Best,
[Artist Name]
[Website/Social Link]

### Submission Tracker Fields
For every station you contact, log:
Station | Contact Name | Genre | Format Sent | Date Sent | Status

## Social Media Plan — Pre-Release Content (7 to 14 Days Before Release)

REELS and TIKTOK content ideas for pre-release:
- Talking reel: artist speaks directly to camera about what inspired the song. Keep it authentic and unscripted.
- Creation process reel: studio footage, writing process, or recording session moments
- Hook clip: the strongest 15 to 30 second moment from the song identified in the analysis
- Lyrics reveal: one powerful lyric line shown as text on screen with the song audio

STORIES content for pre-release:
- Countdown sticker set to the release date
- Poll: Have you heard anything like this before? Yes / No
- Question box: Ask me anything about the new song
- Behind the scenes clips in vertical format
- Swipe up or link sticker pointing to pre-save on Spotify

STATIC POSTS for pre-release:
- Cover art reveal post
- A mood visual with the text "something is coming"
- Artist photo that matches the song's aesthetic

## Social Media Plan — Post-Release 5 Day Intensive

Day 1 after release:
- Reel: share first listener reactions and comments
- Story: screenshot and share every tag and mention you receive
- Thank supporters personally in Stories

Day 2 after release:
- Talking reel: the personal story behind the song in more depth
- Story: "Did you know this song was about..." with one surprising or personal fact

Day 3 after release:
- Lyrics reel or a quote graphic using the most powerful line
- Story poll: "Which line hits hardest?" — show two lyric options for fans to choose

Day 4 after release:
- Reel: directly ask fans to share the song and explain that sharing in the first week matters most for the algorithm
- Story: "Share this to your story if this song describes how you feel"

Day 5 after release:
- Playlist reel: ask fans to add the song to their personal playlists and explain why playlist saves help visibility
- Story: share Spotify stream count update if the numbers are positive

## Social Media Budget Strategy

Apply the correct budget tier based on the artist's total marketing budget:

Budget €0 to €20: 100% organic strategy only. Focus on TikTok organic reach and personal sharing. No paid advertising. Every euro saved here should go toward better cover art.

Budget €20 to €100: Boost one Instagram post on release day. Target a lookalike audience based on similar artists in the same genre. Do not spread the budget across multiple platforms.

Budget €100 to €500: Split the budget across Meta ads (60%) and YouTube pre-roll ads (40%). This combination reaches both passive listeners and active music searchers.

Budget €500 and above: Run a full campaign across Meta, YouTube, and TikTok Spark Ads. TikTok Spark Ads amplify organic posts that are already performing rather than creating new ad content from scratch.

## Social Media Profile Audit — Before Every Release

Before any release, the artist must update every active platform profile.

INSTAGRAM requirements:
- Bio: clear genre and artist identity written in 2 lines maximum
- Profile photo: professional or visually on-brand
- Highlights: create or update covers for Music, Behind the Scenes, Press, and Shows
- Link in bio: must point directly to the new song as the first link. Recommend Linktree or Beacons.ai (both free). First link equals new song on Spotify or YouTube. Second link equals all streaming platforms page. Third link equals contact or booking.

TIKTOK requirements:
- Bio updated to mention the new release
- Link in bio updated to point to the new song

YOUTUBE requirements:
- Channel description updated to mention the new release
- Channel banner updated with release date if technically possible

FACEBOOK requirements:
- About section updated with current release information
- Release announcement post pinned to the top of the page

IMPORTANT RULE for Linktree and Beacons users: If the artist already has a Linktree or Beacons page set up, always tell them: "Move your new song to the FIRST link at the top before release day. This is the single most important link in bio update you can make before the release."

## Platform-Specific Strategy Notes

Spotify: The save rate (ratio of listeners who save the song) is more important than raw stream count in the first week. A high save rate signals to Spotify's algorithm that the song has strong listener intent and can trigger algorithmic playlist additions.

TikTok: The algorithm rewards the first 24 to 48 hours of posting heavily. Post the hook clip at the moment of release, not the day before. Engage with every comment in the first hour after posting to boost the algorithm signal.

Instagram Reels: Cross-post TikTok content to Reels but remove the TikTok watermark first. Use a tool like SnapTik or download from TikTok before the watermark is applied. Watermarked TikTok videos are deprioritized in Instagram's algorithm.

YouTube: Upload the video as unlisted 2 to 3 days before release. Schedule it to go public automatically at the exact release time. This avoids last-minute upload errors and gives YouTube time to process the video properly before it goes live.

## Common Artist Mistakes to Flag

Flag these mistakes proactively if you detect them in the artist's plan:

- Uploading to the distributor less than 7 days before release: this risks the song not being live on release day
- Skipping the Spotify editorial pitch: pitching is free and takes 10 minutes. Never skip it.
- Not scheduling the YouTube video as unlisted in advance: last-minute uploads cause technical errors on release day
- Watermarked TikTok videos cross-posted to Instagram Reels: these get less reach
- Posting the release announcement after 10am on release day: post at or before 9am for maximum early traction
- Not updating the link in bio before release day: fans who click will find old content
- Spending the entire budget on one platform: always split across at least two platforms for budgets above €100
